1. Dynamic Warm-up & Activation

2. Main Lift: Power Snatch

Focus on an aggressive, stable lockout. The weight should be light enough that every rep is fast and crisp.

    • PUNCH THE CEILING: Don't just catch the bar. Actively and violently punch your hands up into the bar to meet it. This is the key to a strong lockout.
    • SHOW ME YOUR ARMPITS: At the top, externally rotate your shoulders so your armpits face forward. This engages your lats and creates a solid shelf.
    • HEAD THROUGH THE WINDOW: Once stable, push your head slightly forward through the "window" your arms create to align your body under the bar.

3. Secondary Lift: Dumbbell Bench Press

    • SHOULDER BLADES PINCHED: Squeeze your shoulder blades together and down, as if you're trying to tuck them into your back pockets. This creates a stable platform to press from.
    • DRIVE FEET DOWN: Keep your feet flat on the floor and maintain tension by thinking about driving them through the ground. This creates full-body tightness.

4. Accessory & Unilateral Work

This is our focus area for fixing the hip shift. Go slow and controlled. Quality over everything.

    • TRIPOD FOOT: "Grip" the floor with your big toe, little toe, and heel. This creates an arch and stabilizes your knee.
    • ELEVATOR, NOT ESCALATOR: Think about your torso going straight down and straight up. Don't let yourself lean forward as you get tired.
    • FOCUS ON THE LEFT: Be extremely mindful of your left leg. Feel the glute fire to control the descent and drive you back up.
